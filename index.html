HISTORY OF US WOMEN'S TENNIS

United States women's tennis has a long and storied history. From breaking barriers from the likes of Althea Gibson to Billie Jean King women's tennis has reflected the changes in American society. The last thirty years has seen the likes of Chris Everett, Martina Navratilova, Lindsay Davenport, Monica Seles.

While these Hall of Fame players helped shaped the game, perhaps none have been so impactful as Venus and Serena Williams. They have combined for a total of 30 Grand Slam singles titles, 14 Grand Slam doubles titles, 4 Grand Slam mixed doubles, 2 Olympic gold medals in singles and 2 gold medals in doubles.

The future of women's tennis in the United States is very bright. Williams sisters alove of tennis has kept them in the game while newbies such as Sofia Kenin and Amanda Anisimova have the grit and tenacity to challenge for the most prestigious trophies on the WTA tour.
